BioSure Phosphate (Solubilizing Bacteria)

Phosphate solubilizing bacteria (PSB) are a group of beneficial bacteria that play an important role in improving soil health and plant growth. These bacteria are capable of solubilizing insoluble phosphate compounds, such as rock phosphate, making these essential nutrients readily available to plants.

In recent years, PSB has gained significant attention in agriculture due to its potential applications in improving crop yields and reducing the need for synthetic fertilizers. By introducing PSB to crops, farmers in India and around the world can potentially improve crop growth, increase crop yields, and reduce the need for synthetic fertilizers.

In addition to its phosphate-solubilizing properties, PSB is also known to produce a variety of growth-promoting substances, such as indole acetic acid, gibberellins, and cytokinins, which are important plant hormones that stimulate root development, enhance nutrient uptake, and increase biomass production. By harnessing the power of PSB, farmers in India can potentially improve their crop yields and incomes, while also promoting more sustainable and environmentally responsible agriculture.
