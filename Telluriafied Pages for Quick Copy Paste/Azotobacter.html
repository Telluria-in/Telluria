BioSure Azotobacter

Azotobacter is a genus of bacteria that is commonly found in soil and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen. Nitrogen fixation is a process by which atmospheric nitrogen is converted into a form that can be taken up by plants, making it an important factor in crop growth and development. In agriculture, Azotobacter has gained significant attention in recent years due to its potential applications in promoting plant growth and improving crop yields.

Azotobacter is known to produce a variety of growth-promoting substances, such as indole acetic acid, which is an important plant hormone that stimulates root development and enhances nutrient uptake. By introducing Azotobacter to crops, farmers in India and around the world can potentially improve crop growth, increase crop yields, and reduce the need for synthetic fertilizers.

In addition to its growth-promoting properties, Azotobacter has also been found to improve the tolerance of crops to environmental stress, such as drought and salinity. This can be especially beneficial for farmers in India, where climate change is leading to more frequent and severe environmental stressors that can negatively impact crop production.
