BioSure Potash (Mobilizing Bacteria)

Potash mobilizing bacteria (PMB) are a group of beneficial bacteria that play a critical role in soil health and plant growth. These bacteria are capable of solubilizing insoluble potash compounds, such as mica and feldspar, and making them readily available to plants.

In agriculture, the use of PMB has gained significant attention in recent years due to its potential applications in improving crop yields and reducing the need for synthetic fertilizers. By introducing PMB to crops, farmers in India and around the world can potentially improve crop growth, increase crop yields, and reduce the need for synthetic fertilizers.

In addition to its potash-mobilizing properties, PMB is also known to produce a range of plant growth-promoting substances such as phytohormones, siderophores, and enzymes that can improve nutrient uptake and overall plant growth. By using PMB-based solutions, farmers in India can potentially improve the health of their soil, increase the fertility of their land, and reduce their dependence on synthetic fertilizers.
